Kinematics in Ring Coordinates

The six kinematic formulas — time, displacement, velocity — derived in Z₂₅₆ ring coordinates via the 16/45 codec. Proven results only · May 2026

Starting Point

Three standard kinematic formulas — one equation, three arrangements:

Standard kinematics
v = d / t
d = v · t
t = d / v

These form a closed definitional loop. In standard physics, t enters as a primitive with no geometric grounding. MPRC replaces t with a ring coordinate derived from QH4 geometry.

The Codec — g and g⁻¹

2.1 Origin of 720

The u-spinor requires 720° for one full cycle. 720 is the moon cycle counted in hours. All time units derive from 720:

Spinor closure identities
720 hours = 1 moon cycle = 1 full spinor traversal
720 / 30 = 24 hours = 1 day
720 × 2 = 1440 = minutes per day
720 × 120 = 86400 = seconds per day
86400 / 120 = 720 ← seconds close back to spinor exactly

720 is the fixed point. Every operation returns to 720 or produces a time unit. The second is the smallest unit where this closure holds.

The QH4 ring has τ = 256 states per traversal. One traversal = 720 hours. The ratio:

16 is the QH4 geometric constant. It factors out of 720 exactly: 720 = 16 × 45.

6.1 The Division Algorithm

The ring codec has no floats. The fraction 2/675 is expressed as the division algorithm:

Division algorithm
2 × t = state × SCALE_n + r
where:
state ∈ {0..255} (ring position)
r ∈ {0..SCALE_n − 1} (sub-state residual)
SCALE_n = 675 × 1024ⁿ

6.2 Forward and Inverse

Integer encode / decode
state = (2 × t) / SCALE_n mod 256 [integer division]
r = (2 × t) mod SCALE_n [remainder]
t = (state × SCALE_n + r) / 2 [exact — sum always even]
Division by 2 in the inverse is always exact. The sum state × SCALE_n + r = 2t is always even by construction.
